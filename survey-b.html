The Scenario

You will watch short video clips of four robots — called BLUE, RED, YELLOW, and GREEN — navigating a shared 2D space. Each robot starts at a random position and must reach its own target location, shown as a matching-color marker on screen.


The robots have no cameras or proximity sensors — they cannot directly see or detect each other. Instead, they have learned to exchange short wireless signals with their teammates. Using these signals, each robot decides at every moment how to move: go straight toward its goal, curve around a teammate, or slow down to avoid a collision.


What "Influence" Means

Because the robots are blind to each other, each robot must decide whose signal to rely on most at any given moment. We call this influence: how much one robot's wireless signal shapes another robot's movement decision right now. A robot that is directly in another's path tends to have high influence on it; a robot that is far away or already parked tends to have little. Influence changes over time as the robots move.

How to Use the Video Player

Playback controls

  • ▶ Play / ⏸ Pause — start or pause the animation
  • ◂ Step / Step ▸ — jump backward or forward exactly one timestep; useful for examining a specific moment closely
  • Slider bar — drag to jump to any point in the episode instantly
  • Speed buttons (0.5× 1× 2× 4×) — control how fast the animation plays; the default is 4×; use 0.5× or 1× to slow down and catch fine details

Display options

  • Trails — shows the path each robot has traveled so far as a faded colored line
  • Goals — shows each robot's target location as a small × marker in its color
  • Grid — shows a coordinate grid in the background
  • Interpolate — smooths movement between timesteps for a more fluid animation

What you see on screen

  • Each colored circle is one robot (BLUE, YELLOW, GREEN, RED)
  • The arrow on a circle shows its current direction and speed — a longer arrow means faster movement
  • The × marker in each color is that robot's destination
